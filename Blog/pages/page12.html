THE ART ADVISOR

Encouraging The Art of Viewing Art

VIRTUALITY BECOMES REALITY
April 16, 2015


Phantom (kingdom of all the animals and all the beasts is my name), 2014–15 by Daniel Steegmann Mangrané is currently displayed in The New Museum’s Triennial: “Surround Audience.” Such piece embodies symbols and notions that pertain to site specificity, appropriation, global environment and video art, which are key concepts that pertain to contemporary art.

As opposed to traditional art viewing, where canvases hang against the walls, this three-dimensional artwork is located in a site-specific space that allows the viewer to explore the whereabouts. Well-known contemporary artist Daniel Steegmann Mangrané presents an empty space with a hanging virtual reality headset strapped from the ceiling and four motion detectors situated on the upper corners of the walls that follow the viewer’s movement.
